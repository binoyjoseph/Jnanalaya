The 16th Chapter of the Bhagavad Gita, 24th Shloka says:

तस्माच्छास्त्रं प्रमाणं ते कार्याकार्यव्यवस्थितौ |
ज्ञात्वा शास्त्रविधानोक्तं कर्म कर्तुमिहार्हसि || 24||

tasmāch chhāstraṁ pramāṇaṁ te kāryākārya-vyavasthitau
jñātvā śhāstra-vidhānoktaṁ karma kartum ihārhasi

Therefore, let the scriptures be your authority in determining what should be done and what should not be done.
Understand the scriptural injunctions and teachings, and then perform your actions in this world accordingly.

Quoting this 24th Shloka in the 16th Chapter of the Bhagavad Gita, His Divine Holiness Sri Paramahamsa Nithyananda articulated his vision of a Vaidika Grantha Samadhi. It is a vision of an internet based Library, which he called as Nithyananda Jnanalaya Hindu Scripture.


In our continuous effort to preserve and make digital collection available to researchers, seekers, We have collected more than 15TB of digital Manuscript and rare books. We are continuously collecting books and manuscripts from around the globe.

We thank all volunteers, organization who shared this valuable digital collection and allowed us to make it available online for reference and
research purpose.

Who can donate digital Collection?

We encourage anyone who has books on Hinduism, Buddhism, Jainism and Sikkhism, Can donate digital collection to us. We also accept physical collection of books on above branches. You can also contact us for digitization of such books. Please click here to contact us.

What type of Books and Manuscripts, do we collect?

We collect physical and Digital collection of Manuscripts and rare Books. All these collection must belong to any Sampradaya of Hinduism, Buddhism, Sikhism and Jainism. We also collect books on Temples, temple architecture, Indian Art and architecture, Indian Cooking, Archeology, Indian performing Arts, traditional folk arts, languages, Hindu History, Indian Dress or fashion, Hindu Literature, archaeology and many more.
